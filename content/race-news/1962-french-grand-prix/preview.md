### Championship standings

Hill leads with 16 points, ahead of Phil Hill on 14 and Clark on 9. In the Manufacturers' Championship, BRM leads on 16, Lotus 15, Ferrari 14.

### Previous race

Clark took his first Formula One victory at Spa, with Hill second and Phil Hill third. On the final lap Taylor and Mairesse collided fighting for second — the Lotus cut down a telegraph pole, the Ferrari landed upside down on fire, and both drivers were flung clear with minor injuries. Ricardo Rodríguez finished fourth, becoming the youngest driver in championship history to score points at 20 years and 123 days.

### Entrants

Ferrari has withdrawn due to ongoing metalworkers' strikes in Italy. Phil Hill watches from the grandstands. Despite the absence of Ferrari, the first three rows of the grid are filled by seven different teams across five constructors.

### Track changes

The French Grand Prix returns to Rouen-Les-Essarts for the first time since 1957, following the traditional five-year rotation away from Reims-Gueux.
