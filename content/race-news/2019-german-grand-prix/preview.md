### Championship standings

Hamilton leads Bottas by 39 points. Mercedes leads Ferrari by 164 in the Constructors'. Both championships will remain led by the current leaders regardless of this weekend's result.

### Previous race

Hamilton won at Silverstone for a record sixth time at his home race, equalling Alain Prost's record for the most wins at a single Grand Prix. He set the fastest lap on the final lap running the hardest available tyre — a compound he had used for most of the race. Vettel locked up and crashed into the back of Verstappen at Vale corner while battling for third, earning a 10-second penalty and finishing 16th.

### Car upgrades

Mercedes run a special one-off livery this weekend to mark their 125th year competing in motorsport and their 200th Formula One start. Racing Point bring a two-step upgrade package. Williams also introduce upgrades. Haas are running split car setups for the second consecutive race after the British GP inter-team collision, with Magnussen's car carrying additional upgrades.

### Penalties

Vettel is required to start from the back of the grid for fitting a third set of control electronics. He failed to set a Q1 time within the 107% requirement but has been permitted to race at the stewards' discretion; the penalty makes no difference to his starting position.

### Practice

Vettel was fastest in FP1 ahead of Leclerc and Hamilton; a brief red flag was caused by a car failure for Magnussen. FP2 saw Leclerc fastest again, Vettel just over a tenth behind in second, with Hamilton third. Gasly crashed on the exit of turn 16 during FP2.
