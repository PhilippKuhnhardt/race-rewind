### Championship standings

Schumacher leads with 86 points, 54 clear of Barrichello in second and 55 ahead of Montoya in third. Ralf Schumacher is fourth on 30, Coulthard fifth on 26. Ferrari lead the Constructors' by 57 points over Williams.

The race at Magny-Cours is the season's eleventh round — also the 11th anniversary of the circuit's first Formula One event. The French and British Grands Prix swapped places this year to avoid conflict with the FIFA World Cup finals in Japan and South Korea.

### Championship permutations

If Schumacher wins here and both Barrichello and Montoya finish lower than third, he will have matched Juan Manuel Fangio's record of five world championships.

### Previous race

Schumacher won in Britain as rain split strategies in the second half of the race. Bridgestone's intermediate compound outperformed Michelin's full wets in the wet conditions, giving Schumacher and Barrichello a decisive pace advantage. Barrichello, starting from the back after stalling on the formation lap, drove through to second. Montoya third. BAR scored their first points of the season with both cars, Villeneuve fourth and Panis fifth.

### Between-race developments

Teams tested variously at four circuits over the fortnight. Arrows nearly did not participate — team manager Tom Walkinshaw directed the transporters from the Leafield factory to France on 16 July, and the team's debt to Cosworth was paid off only on 17 July, before a noon deadline. Arrows will appear in qualifying but did not take part in Friday practice while negotiations with shareholder Morgan Grenfell continued.

### Car upgrades

Ferrari combines the rear wing mounting point with the deformable structure above the gearbox and updates the extractor profile. Williams introduces large sidepod openings to improve heat dissipation in the expected high temperatures. Renault debuts a new, more powerful engine version and an aperture to cool the right-hand oil radiator.

### Practice

Four sessions in hot, sunny weather with Schumacher fastest across all four. In Friday's first session, Sato cut across Salo at the fast right/left Imola turn, causing a collision; Salo returned to the pits with front suspension damage but continued. Arrows sat out both Friday sessions under management instruction. In the second session, Trulli took too much kerb through the Nürburgring chicane and nudged the tyre barrier, ending his running.

Saturday's final session produced the weekend's most significant incident: Fisichella's front wing collapsed over the kerbs at the exit of the Estoril corner. The wing folded under the car, lifting the front wheels off the track; Fisichella hit the outside tyre barrier at 125 mph under a 34g deceleration. Practice was stopped for 26 minutes. Fisichella was taken to hospital with a concussion, a cut finger, and a stiff neck; FIA medical delegate Sid Watkins has deemed him unfit to race.

With Fisichella unable to compete and Arrows having failed the 107% rule, 18 cars are set to start — the fewest since the 1975 Monaco Grand Prix.
