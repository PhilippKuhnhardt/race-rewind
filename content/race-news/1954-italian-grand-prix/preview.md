### Previous race

At Bremgarten, Fangio led González home by nearly a minute, lapping the entire field up to second place. Moss and Hawthorn fought fiercely for position behind the leaders until both retired. The result was conclusive: with González unable to win, Fangio clinched his second World Championship.

### Championship standings

Fangio is the 1954 World Champion. González holds second in the standings, with Trintignant third. One round remains after Monza — the season finale in Spain — and the runners-up positions are still to be decided.

### Entrants

Alberto Ascari lines up alongside Fangio on the front row, driving for Ferrari — Lancia still have not completed the D50 for championship use. Stirling Moss starts from the second row for the Maserati works team.
