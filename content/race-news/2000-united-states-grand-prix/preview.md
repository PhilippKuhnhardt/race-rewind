### Championship standings

Häkkinen leads with 80 points, Schumacher second on 78. Schumacher won in Italy to close the gap to two points. Three races remain with 30 points available. McLaren lead the Constructors' Championship by four points.

### Previous race

At Monza, Schumacher won his 41st career victory — equalling Ayrton Senna's record of wins. When a journalist informed him of this in the post-race press conference, he burst into tears; once he had composed himself, he said he remembered Senna's fatal accident at the 1994 San Marino Grand Prix. The race itself was overshadowed by a first-lap accident in which fire marshal Paolo Ghislimberti was struck by a wheel and killed. Häkkinen finished second, closing the gap from six points to two. Häkkinen had won three of the previous five races; this is a championship that is going to the final round.

### Circuit

This is the United States Grand Prix's return to Formula One after a nine-year absence. The road circuit is built inside the Indianapolis Motor Speedway, ending construction just two weeks before the event. The banked turn 13 is the circuit's defining feature — the only section where F1 cars will use the oval. Most drivers praised the layout; Villeneuve was critical, calling the infield section "very slow." 1978 World Champion Mario Andretti tested the track beforehand. All but one team has never raced here.
