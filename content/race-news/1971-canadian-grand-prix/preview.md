### Championship standings

Both the Drivers' and Constructors' Championships are settled. Stewart is World Champion and Tyrrell have won the Constructors' Cup.

### Previous race

Italy: The race at Monza produced the closest finish in Formula One history — five cars covered by 0.61 seconds at the line. It was the fastest-ever Formula One race, with an average speed of 150.75 mph. Jean-Pierre Jarier and Mike Hailwood made their debuts; Hailwood, the motorcycle world champion, held both the Formula 5000 and motorcycle lap records at Monza.

### Between-race developments

Tyrrell secured the Constructors' Championship at Monza with two races remaining.

### Track changes

The Canadian Grand Prix moves from Mont-Tremblant to Mosport Park near Toronto.

### Entrants

Mark Donohue makes his Formula One debut, driving a Penske-entered McLaren. Chris Craft also makes his debut.

### Milestones

This is Matra's 50th Grand Prix start as a constructor.
