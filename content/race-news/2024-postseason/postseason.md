### Season summary

The 2024 FIA Formula One World Championship was the 75th running of the championship, contested over a record twenty-four Grands Prix. Defending Drivers' Champion Max Verstappen of Red Bull Racing dominated the opening 10 races with seven wins, but the season was highly competitive with a record-making six other drivers claiming multiple Grand Prix victories. McLaren driver Lando Norris emerged as Verstappen's closest competitor as the MCL38 began to consistently outperform the RB20. Verstappen maintained his points advantage to win his fourth consecutive Drivers' Championship title at the Las Vegas Grand Prix.

Verstappen's title made him only the third drivers' champion after Keke Rosberg in 1982 and Nelson Piquet in 1983 to win the drivers' championship with a team that finished third or lower in the constructors' championship, and the second driver after Piquet to win the drivers' championship in a team that did not become constructors' champions on two or more occasions. McLaren surpassed Red Bull and achieved their ninth Constructors' Championship title at the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix, narrowly ahead of Ferrari by 14 points. With their first Constructors' Championship in 26 years, McLaren became the first constructor other than Red Bull and Mercedes to win the title since Brawn in 2009.

### Final Drivers' Championship

Max Verstappen finished with 437 points, securing his fourth consecutive title at Las Vegas. Lando Norris was runner-up with 374 points, ahead of Charles Leclerc with 356. Oscar Piastri (292), Carlos Sainz Jr. (290), George Russell (245), Lewis Hamilton (223), and Sergio Pérez (152) completed the top eight.

### Final Constructors' Championship

McLaren won their ninth Constructors' Championship — their first since 1998 — with 666 points. Ferrari finished second with 652, Red Bull third with 589. Mercedes (468), Aston Martin (94), Alpine (65), Haas (58), RB (46), Williams (28), and Kick Sauber (4) completed the standings. Sauber scored their first and only points of the season at Qatar through Zhou Guanyu's eighth-place finish.

### In-season driver changes

Carlos Sainz Jr. withdrew from the Saudi Arabian Grand Prix with appendicitis and was replaced by Oliver Bearman, who scored points on his Formula One debut. Kevin Magnussen was banned for the Azerbaijan Grand Prix after reaching 12 penalty points in 12 months; Bearman stood in again and scored a point for Haas. Bearman also replaced an unwell Magnussen for the São Paulo Grand Prix weekend.

Franco Colapinto replaced Logan Sargeant at Williams from the Italian Grand Prix onwards, scoring five points and becoming the first Argentine driver to score since Carlos Reutemann in 1982. Liam Lawson replaced Daniel Ricciardo at RB from the United States Grand Prix onwards. Jack Doohan made his debut at the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix, replacing Esteban Ocon at Alpine.

### Notable statistics

- Bearman became the first driver in history to score points for two different teams (Ferrari, Haas) in his first two Formula One races.
- Norris took his maiden victory at Miami, McLaren's first win since the 2021 Italian Grand Prix. Piastri took his maiden victory at Hungary in McLaren's first 1–2 finish since 2021.
- Hamilton won his 104th career race and a record ninth British Grand Prix at Silverstone, also becoming the first driver to win after his 300th start.
- At the Belgian Grand Prix, George Russell became the first driver disqualified from a Grand Prix victory since Michael Schumacher in 1994.
- Colapinto was the first Argentine to score points in Formula One since 1982.
- Zhou scored Sauber's sole points of the season at Qatar.
- Esteban Ocon scored his first career fastest lap and Alpine's first-ever fastest lap on his 152nd start at the United States Grand Prix.
- Yuki Tsunoda became the first Japanese driver to score points at their home race since Kamui Kobayashi's podium in 2012.
- Hamilton's move to Ferrari for 2025 was announced during the season, ending the most successful driver-team partnership in Formula One history.

### Farewells

The Abu Dhabi Grand Prix marked Lewis Hamilton's final race for Mercedes before moving to Ferrari. Carlos Sainz Jr. completed his final race for Ferrari before joining Williams. Sergio Pérez concluded his stint with Red Bull Racing. Kevin Magnussen retired from Formula One after a decade. Valtteri Bottas and Zhou Guanyu departed Sauber ahead of the team's transition to Audi.
