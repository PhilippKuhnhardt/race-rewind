### Season summary

The 2024 FIA Formula One World Championship was the 75th running of the championship, contested over a record twenty-four Grands Prix.

### Red Bull's fade

Red Bull Racing entered the season as reigning double champions and looked imperious early on, taking 1-2 finishes at Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, and Japan. Verstappen won seven of the first ten races, building a commanding points lead. But a mid-season performance decline saw the RB20 lose its edge. Verstappen went ten races without a win between Spain and São Paulo -- the longest winless streak of his career since becoming a regular contender. Sergio Pérez's struggles compounded Red Bull's decline; his qualifying and race pace deteriorated sharply from Monaco onwards, and the team dropped from first to third in the Constructors' Championship.

### McLaren's ascent

McLaren's MCL38 improved dramatically through the season. Lando Norris took his maiden victory at Miami in round six, and by the summer break McLaren had overtaken Ferrari for second in the constructors' standings. Oscar Piastri claimed his maiden win at Hungary in a McLaren 1-2, the team's first since the 2021 Italian Grand Prix. After the break, Norris won by over twenty-two seconds at Zandvoort, and McLaren took the constructors' lead at Azerbaijan -- their first time atop the standings since 2014.

### The title fights

Despite McLaren's superiority in the second half, Verstappen's early-season cushion proved decisive. He clinched his fourth consecutive Drivers' Championship at Las Vegas with two races to spare, finishing fifth while Norris could only manage sixth. Verstappen became only the third champion -- after Keke Rosberg in 1982 and Nelson Piquet in 1983 -- to win the drivers' title while his team finished third or lower in the constructors' championship.

The Constructors' Championship went down to the final race. McLaren secured their ninth title and first since 1998 at Abu Dhabi, where Norris controlled the race from pole. They finished just 14 points ahead of Ferrari, who mounted a late charge with four victories in the final eight races. McLaren became the first constructor other than Red Bull and Mercedes to win the title since Brawn in 2009.

### Seven winners

A record six drivers other than Verstappen won races: Norris (4), Leclerc (3), Sainz (2), Piastri (2), Russell (2), and Hamilton (2). Ferrari won at home in Monza for the first time since 2019. Mercedes took back-to-back victories at Austria and Silverstone, ending a winless run stretching to the 2022 São Paulo Grand Prix. Hamilton's Silverstone win -- his 104th and record ninth at the circuit -- came after his 300th start, making him the first driver to win that deep into a career.

### Final Drivers' Championship

Max Verstappen finished with 437 points, securing his fourth consecutive title at Las Vegas. Lando Norris was runner-up with 374 points, ahead of Charles Leclerc with 356. Oscar Piastri (292), Carlos Sainz Jr. (290), George Russell (245), Lewis Hamilton (223), and Sergio Pérez (152) completed the top eight.

### Final Constructors' Championship

McLaren won their ninth Constructors' Championship — their first since 1998 — with 666 points. Ferrari finished second with 652, Red Bull third with 589. Mercedes (468), Aston Martin (94), Alpine (65), Haas (58), RB (46), Williams (28), and Kick Sauber (4) completed the standings. Sauber scored their first and only points of the season at Qatar through Zhou Guanyu's eighth-place finish.
