### Championship standings

Graham Hill leads on 30 points, closely followed by Jacky Ickx on 27, Jackie Stewart on 26, and defending champion Denny Hulme on 24. The opening race of a three-round North American finale determines whether this remains a four-way fight or narrows further.

### Previous race

Hulme won a slipstreaming battle at Monza, with Johnny Servoz-Gavin second for Matra and Ickx third for Ferrari. Hill retired when a wheel came off his Lotus. Stewart led late in the race but dropped out with engine failure, allowing Hulme to win; Siffert followed Stewart out with a rear suspension failure nine laps from the end.

### Entrants

The race moves from Mosport Park to the Circuit Mont-Tremblant at St. Jovite in Quebec, one and a half hours northwest of Montreal. Canadian driver Bill Brack appears in a third Lotus, while Dan Gurney runs a third McLaren (entered by his Anglo American Racers team) having abandoned his Eagle-Weslake programme. A privately entered Eagle T1F appears in the hands of local Formula A/5000 driver Al Pease. BRM run a single car for Rodríguez. Matra Sports expand to two cars, with Henri Pescarolo joining Beltoise; alongside Stewart and Servoz-Gavin in the Matra International entries, that makes four Matras in the field.

### Milestones

Bill Brack and Henri Pescarolo make their Formula One World Championship debuts.

### Practice

Jochen Rindt took pole position for Brabham — the new Repco V8 engine finally delivering competitive pace — with Amon's Ferrari and Siffert's Lotus alongside on the front row. Gurney qualified on the second row alongside Hill.
