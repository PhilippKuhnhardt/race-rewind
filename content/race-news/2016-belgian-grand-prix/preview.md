### Championship standings

Hamilton leads Rosberg by 19 points. Red Bull Racing has passed Ferrari in the Constructors' standings.

### Previous race

Hamilton won Germany for his fourth consecutive victory, extending his championship lead to 19 points. Ricciardo finished second ahead of Verstappen. Rosberg slipped to fourth after a time penalty for forcing Verstappen off the track.

### Between-race developments

Rio Haryanto has lost his race seat after his sponsors were unable to meet their financial obligations to MRT. He remains with the team in a testing and reserve role. Esteban Ocon, the 2015 GP3 Series champion and Renault test driver, replaces him and makes his Formula One debut.

### Penalties

Hamilton receives a 60-place grid penalty — effectively starting last — after Mercedes deliberately introduces multiple new power unit components, including a seventh turbocharger and MGU-H. Mercedes regards Spa's overtaking opportunities as sufficient to offset the penalties and wants to stockpile components for the remainder of the season. Fernando Alonso also receives a 60-place penalty for McLaren's sixth complete power unit. Marcus Ericsson carries a ten-place penalty for a new turbocharger.

Esteban Gutiérrez carries a five-place penalty for dangerous driving in FP3 when Pascal Wehrlein was forced to cross the grassy verge inside Raidillon to avoid contact.

On race day, Mercedes removes a seal on Hamilton's gearbox so he can fit a new gearbox at the next race, adding a further five-place penalty.

### Tyres

Pirelli brings the super-soft, soft and medium compounds. Teams also receive two sets of "prototype" soft tyres for use in practice — a more durable version designed to trial improvements ahead of their competitive debut in Malaysia.

### Car upgrades

Both Honda and Mercedes have introduced power unit upgrades. Sauber brings their first chassis updates of the season to the C35.
