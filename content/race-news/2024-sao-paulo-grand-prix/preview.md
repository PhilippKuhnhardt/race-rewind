### Championship standings before the race

Max Verstappen leads the Drivers' Championship with 362 points, 47 points ahead of Lando Norris in second, and 71 ahead of Charles Leclerc in third. McLaren, holding 566 points, enters this round as the leader of the Constructors' Championship from Ferrari and Red Bull Racing, who are second and third with 537 and 512 points, respectively. Carlos Sainz Jr. is mathematically eliminated from championship contention.

### Entrants

Franco Colapinto continues at Williams, having replaced Logan Sargeant from the Italian Grand Prix onwards. Liam Lawson continues at RB, having replaced Daniel Ricciardo from the United States Grand Prix. Reserve driver Oliver Bearman replaces Kevin Magnussen at Haas for the free practice and sprint sessions as Magnussen feels unwell. This is later expanded to the rest of the weekend following sprint qualifying.

### Between-race developments

At the Mexico City Grand Prix, Sainz won comfortably for his fourth career win, with Norris second and Leclerc third. The result allowed Ferrari to take second place ahead of Red Bull Racing in the Constructors' Championship. Verstappen received two separate ten-second penalties for leaving the track and gaining an advantage while battling Norris, allowing Norris to close the championship gap to 47 points. Sergio Pérez endured a difficult home race, finishing last out of all finishing cars after a collision with Lawson and a false start penalty.

### Sprint weekend format

This is the fifth of six Grands Prix in the season to utilise the sprint format. The weekend structure consists of a single free practice session, followed by sprint qualifying, the sprint race on Saturday morning, then Grand Prix qualifying on Saturday afternoon, with the main race on Sunday. This is the fourth time overall that the São Paulo Grand Prix has featured the sprint format.

### Tyre choices

Tyre supplier Pirelli brings the C3, C4, and C5 tyre compounds (the softest three in their range), designated hard, medium, and soft, respectively.

The only free practice session is topped by Lando Norris of McLaren ahead of George Russell of Mercedes and Oliver Bearman of Haas.
