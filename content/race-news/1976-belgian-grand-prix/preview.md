### Championship standings

The Spanish Grand Prix result remains under appeal. Hunt crossed the line first but was disqualified in post-race scrutineering after his McLaren was found to be 1.5 cm too wide. McLaren have appealed, arguing the excess width was caused by tyre expansion during the race. Until the appeal is resolved, Lauda is the declared winner and his championship lead stands at 23 points over Hunt.

### Previous race

Hunt took the lead from Lauda midway through Spain and appeared to win, but was excluded after scrutineering. Lauda was declared the winner ahead of Nilsson and Reutemann. McLaren's appeal is ongoing.

### Entrants

Zolder hosts the Belgian Grand Prix this year. Nivelles-Baulers, due to alternate with Zolder, had its track surface deteriorate to the point that it could not be used.

### Milestones

A Ford-powered car has finished on the podium in every World Championship race since the 1971 Dutch Grand Prix — a run of 67 consecutive podiums. That streak is at risk this weekend, with Ferrari locking out the front row.
