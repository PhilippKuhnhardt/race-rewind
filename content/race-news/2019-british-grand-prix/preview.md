### Championship standings

Hamilton leads Bottas by 31 points. Mercedes leads Ferrari by 135 in the Constructors'.

### Previous race

At Austria, Verstappen passed Leclerc for the lead with three laps remaining in a move that made contact but was deemed a racing incident by the stewards. It was the first win for a Honda-powered car since Jenson Button in the 2006 Hungarian Grand Prix, and the first victory of 2019 not taken by a Mercedes driver. Every car finished the race — the first such result since the 2016 Japanese Grand Prix.

### Between-race developments

The race was officially started by Justin Whiting, son of Race Director Charlie Whiting, who died before the season opener in Australia. Sebastian Vettel gave a brief speech in commemoration before the start.

### Practice

Gasly was fastest in FP1, almost half a second clear of Bottas and a further 0.4 seconds ahead of Verstappen. The session was disrupted by two incidents: Grosjean crashed his car at the pit exit, and Räikkönen's Alfa Romeo stopped on the Wellington Straight, bringing out a red flag. The recently resurfaced track — resurfaced for the second time in under 16 months — left all drivers struggling for grip throughout. Brief rain had little impact on the session.
