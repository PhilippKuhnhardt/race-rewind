### Previous race

Canada was decided at the first corner. Piquet qualified on pole but used a fragile qualifying-specification car to do it. Jones outlaunched him at the start, the pair touched, Piquet hit the wall, and a multi-car pile-up followed. Piquet restarted in his qualifying car; the engine lasted only a handful of laps before failing. Jones won and clinched the 1980 Drivers' Championship — the first Australian to win the title since Jack Brabham in 1966, and Williams's first Drivers' title to go alongside the Constructors' crown secured at Imola. Jean-Pierre Jabouille crashed heavily and was cut from his car; he broke both legs.

### Championship

Jones is the 1980 World Champion. Piquet holds second in the standings. Third place remains open: Reutemann leads Laffite by eight points. Second in the constructors' championship is also contested, with Brabham five points behind Ligier.

### Championship permutations

If Piquet wins at Watkins Glen, he would be required to drop the two points from his fifth-place finish at Austria, leaving him one point behind Jones regardless of Jones's result. The constructors' and drivers' titles are both decided.

### Between-race developments

Jean-Pierre Jabouille will miss this race due to his leg injuries from Canada. Mario Andretti wrote off his Lotus at the Canadian Grand Prix; he takes the car originally allocated to Nigel Mansell, who will not compete.

### Background

The Watkins Glen Grand Prix Race Course in New York State — the twentieth and final Formula One race to be held at this venue — very nearly did not take place. The race was in doubt for much of the season until a loan from FOCA to the organisers secured its future. Alain Prost crashed heavily at Turn 10 during Friday practice, hitting his head on the steering wheel and suffering a concussion; he will not race.

### Milestones

This is the final Formula One World Championship race for both Emerson Fittipaldi and Jody Scheckter.
