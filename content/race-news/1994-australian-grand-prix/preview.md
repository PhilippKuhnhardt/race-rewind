### Championship standings

Both championships are decided in Adelaide. Schumacher leads the Drivers' Championship with 92 points; Hill is one point behind on 91. Williams lead the Constructors' Championship with 108 points; Benetton are five behind on 103.

### Previous race

At Suzuka, Hill won in torrential rain to close the gap to one point. The race was stopped on lap 13 after Gianni Morbidelli crashed at the Esses; Martin Brundle spun at the same spot moments later and, as he bounced off the barriers, struck a track marshal attending to Morbidelli's car — the marshal suffered a broken leg. The result was determined on aggregate time. Schumacher had led by 6.8 seconds when the red flag was shown; Hill pitted for fuel on the restart and Schumacher had to make a second stop, giving Hill a 3.3-second aggregate win. Alesi finished third.

### Between-race developments

Jean-Denis Délétraz replaces Érik Comas at Larrousse for this final round. Domenico Schiattarella returns to Simtek after Taki Inoue's one-race deal at Suzuka. Peter Sauber has confirmed a deal with Ford to supply engines to the team for 1995.

### Milestones

This is the final Formula One race for Team Lotus — seven-time Constructors' Champions and one of the most successful constructors in the sport's history.
