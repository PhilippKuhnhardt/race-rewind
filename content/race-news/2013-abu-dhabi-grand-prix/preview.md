### Previous race

Sebastian Vettel clinched his fourth consecutive World Drivers' Championship at the Indian Grand Prix, winning the race with Nico Rosberg second and Romain Grosjean third. Red Bull simultaneously secured the Constructors' Championship. It was Vettel's sixth consecutive victory.

### Milestones

A win in Abu Dhabi would give Vettel seven consecutive victories, equalling the Formula One record held by Alberto Ascari and Michael Schumacher.

### Tyre choices

Pirelli brings the medium (prime) and soft (option) compounds, as at the 2012 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix.

### Practice

Romain Grosjean and Lewis Hamilton topped the first practice session. Red Bull's drivers led the second session, with Vettel ahead of Webber by a tenth of a second. In the third and final session, Vettel finished two-tenths clear of Webber, who in turn was less than a hundredth ahead of Hamilton's Mercedes.
