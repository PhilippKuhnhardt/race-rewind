### Championship standings

Graham Hill and Denny Hulme are level on points going into Watkins Glen with two races remaining. Ickx is out of contention after breaking his leg in Canada.

### Previous race

Hulme and McLaren delivered a 1–2 for the McLaren team at Mont-Tremblant, with Rodríguez third for BRM. Chris Amon led 72 of 90 laps in his Ferrari before his transmission failed on lap 73. Hill dropped from second to fifth as a vibration problem worsened, leaving him level on points with Hulme.

### Between-race developments

Jacky Ickx crashed during qualifying in Canada when his throttle stuck open, breaking his leg. Ferrari replaces him with Derek Bell for Watkins Glen.

### Entrants

Mario Andretti, who practiced at Monza but was banned from starting, is entered in a third Lotus for his first World Championship race start. Bobby Unser returns in the second BRM. McLaren run a third car for Dan Gurney. Henri Pescarolo is entered for Matra Sports but his V12 blew up in practice; he does not start. Jackie Oliver suffered a wheel failure and crashed the second Lotus heavily during practice.

### Practice

On the first practice day, Stewart and Amon traded times, with Stewart posting the session's best. Stewart's car developed a broken stub-axle during the second day and he could not improve on his time. Graham Hill then bumped Amon from second, before Mario Andretti — making his first ever Formula One appearance at a circuit he had not previously seen — put his Lotus on pole by 0.07 seconds over Stewart's best, to the delight of the 93,000-strong crowd. It was the first Formula One pole position of Andretti's career and his first ever start.
