### Championship standings

Stewart leads the championship following his Argentine victory.

### Previous race

At Buenos Aires, Stewart took the lead from Reutemann early and built a commanding margin, finishing almost half a minute ahead of Hulme. The Ferraris of Ickx and Regazzoni completed the top four. Reutemann struggled on his tyres and dropped to fourth by lap 11 before pitting and rejoining a lap down. Fittipaldi retired with suspension damage.

### Entrants

Jean-Pierre Beltoise missed Argentina due to legal proceedings arising from the accident that claimed Ignazio Giunti's life in January 1971; he joins BRM for this race. Mike Hailwood returns to Surtees having also missed Argentina because of a conflicting Formula 5000 race. The Eifelland Caravans team makes its World Championship debut with Rolf Stommelen. Frank Williams enters a second March-Cosworth for Carlos Pace alongside Henri Pescarolo. Local entries include Dave Charlton in a Lucky Strike-sponsored Lotus 72D and Team Gunston cars for John Love and Willie Ferguson.

Stewart takes pole, ahead of Regazzoni and Fittipaldi on row one; Hailwood qualifies fourth.

### Milestones

Carlos Pace and Willie Ferguson make their Formula One World Championship debuts.
