### Championship standings

Schumacher leads the championship with 20 points from two wins, 7 ahead of teammate Barrichello and 8 ahead of Williams driver Juan Pablo Montoya. Ferrari's 33 points in the Constructors' Championship is nearly double Williams's 17, with Renault third on 14.

### Previous race

Schumacher won from pole in Sepang, with Montoya second and Jenson Button third — Button's first career podium, and BAR Honda's best result since the 2001 German Grand Prix. Mark Webber, who had split the Ferraris in qualifying, made a poor start and was eliminated in a collision with Ralf Schumacher.

### Weather

Friday and Saturday ran under clear skies and high temperatures. A desert storm hit the circuit on Sunday morning, bringing first rain and then sand. With the track more slippery than its already-new asphalt made it, and air temperatures down, the Bridgestone runners — most notably Ferrari — are expected to hold an advantage over Michelin-shod teams.

### Practice

Four free practice sessions were held. Schumacher and Barrichello topped the first Friday session; Barrichello led the second by a thousandth of a second from Montoya, with Schumacher fourth. On Saturday, Button topped both sessions in his BAR-Honda — heading Montoya and Schumacher in the third, and the two Williamses in the fourth.

### Penalties

Kimi Räikkönen, Nick Heidfeld, and Zsolt Baumgartner carry ten-place grid penalties for engine changes. McLaren aborted Räikkönen's second qualifying session effort to preserve the Mercedes engine after multiple practice issues.
