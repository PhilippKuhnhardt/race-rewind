### Previous race

At Nivelles, Fittipaldi dominated from pole to flag for his second consecutive win. Cevert drove a strong second for Tyrrell in Stewart's absence; Hulme completed the podium.

### Between-race developments

Stewart returns to the Tyrrell cockpit after missing Belgium with gastritis.

### Track changes

The French Grand Prix moves to the Circuit de Charade near Clermont-Ferrand — a 8.2 km stretch of public roads around the base of an extinct volcano. Dark volcanic rocks fall from the mountain onto both sides of the circuit, making tyre punctures a known hazard. Drivers who run wide send rocks into the middle of the track ahead of pursuing cars.

### Entrants

Patrick Depailler makes his Formula One debut. Chris Amon takes pole position for Matra — the Tyrrell team brought a new-specification chassis for Cevert, who was fastest in practice before crashing into a barrier and injuring his hand; he starts the race in the old-specification spare car. Hulme and Stewart start second and third.

### Milestones

Amon's pole position is the fifth of his Formula One career.
