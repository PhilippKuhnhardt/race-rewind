### Championship standings

Jack Brabham is World Champion. Surtees and Rindt are fighting for second place in the standings.

### Previous race

At Monza, Ludovico Scarfiotti won for Ferrari — the first Italian to win his home Grand Prix since Alberto Ascari in 1952 — with Parkes second and Hulme third, Parkes and Hulme separated by 0.3 seconds. Brabham clinched the championship when Surtees retired with a split fuel tank. Rindt's front tyre deflated on the exit of the final corner; he crossed the line while spinning and ended in the grass.

### Between-race developments

John Taylor, badly burned in a collision with Jacky Ickx at the German Grand Prix, has died from his injuries.

### Milestones

Watkins Glen has introduced a prize money system in place of the traditional starting money arrangement. The total purse of $102,400 is the richest in the World Championship; the first prize alone exceeds the combined first prizes of all other rounds on the calendar.

### Practice

Clark qualified the Lotus 43 with the BRM H16 engine, which was found to be leaking oil immediately after. The BRM team provided a second-hand spare H16, and Lotus mechanics fitted it overnight. On race morning, Clark was still undecided which car to run. He chose the H16-powered Type 43, which was again leaking oil on the dummy grid before the crew sealed it ahead of the warm-up lap.
