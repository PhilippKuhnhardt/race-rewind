### Previous race

At Zandvoort, Lauda won for Ferrari with Regazzoni second. Fittipaldi completed the podium in third.

### Circuit

The French Grand Prix moves to the newly built Dijon-Prenois circuit near Dijon, leaving Charade for the first time. The circuit is so short that twelve drivers set qualifying lap times of under one minute — the only World Championship race in which sub-60-second laps have ever been recorded.

### Entrants

José Dolhem and Jean-Pierre Jabouille make their Formula One World Championship debuts this weekend.

### Qualifying

Niki Lauda takes pole position, with Ronnie Peterson second. Tom Pryce puts in a remarkable performance to qualify third in the Shadow — the best grid position of his career so far.

### Pre-race

To honour the 80th birthday of the Automobile Club de France, a parade of vintage cars is organised featuring drivers from the 1920s and 1930s through to the present day.
