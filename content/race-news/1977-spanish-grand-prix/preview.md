### Previous race

At Long Beach, Andretti held off Scheckter through most of the race before passing him with a clean move into the hairpin on lap 77, winning in the Lotus 78's first victory. Lauda was second, less than a second behind, and Scheckter third after his tyre slowly deflated over the final 18 laps.

### Between-race developments

Lauda is unable to start the Spanish Grand Prix, having broken a rib.

### Entrants

Williams Grand Prix Engineering makes its competitive debut, entering a March 761 for Patrick Nève. The LEC team — running a chassis designed by Mike Pilbeam for David Purley — also makes its championship debut. Hesketh Racing fields 1976 British Formula 3 champion Rupert Keegan, who makes his Formula 1 debut.
