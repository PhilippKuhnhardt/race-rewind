### Championship standings

Brabham leads the championship with 19 points, ahead of Brooks on 14 and Phil Hill on 9.

### Previous race

At Reims, Brooks dominated from the front, leading all 50 laps to win by 27 seconds over Phil Hill. The race was run in extreme heat — 44 °C in the sun — and the bitumen began to melt, with stones from the breaking road surface catching Masten Gregory across the face and forcing him to retire, and a stone holing Graham Hill's radiator. Brabham came home third. Moss was disqualified from eighth after receiving a push-start, having been unable to keep his BRM running with a failing gearbox.

### Between-race developments

Jean Behra was sacked by Ferrari after the French Grand Prix. Following the race, a heated discussion with team manager Romolo Tavoni at a restaurant ended with Behra punching Tavoni and a patron. He will not race for the team again.

Innes Ireland is ill and will be replaced at Lotus by Alan Stacey.

### Entrants

Ferrari is absent from the British Grand Prix. A labour dispute in Italy has trapped the entire team at home, leaving the field to the British constructors. Tony Brooks has been given a release to start in a Vanwall — his old team from 1958 — in an attempt to remain within reach of Brabham in the championship.

This is the largest entry of the 1959 season outside the Indianapolis 500: 30 cars entered with 24 starting. Nearly all the drivers are Anglo-Saxon.

Several teams have entered 1500cc Formula Two cars with Coventry Climax, Borgward, or Maserati engines alongside the full Formula One machinery.
