### Championship standings

Fangio leads the championship by ten points over Ascari heading to Monza.

### Previous race

At the Nürburgring, Ascari took his maiden World Championship race victory, leading from lap five after the Alfas struggled with overheating and gearbox failures. Fangio lost first and second gears and was unable to hold off Ascari in his second stint. González completed the podium; Ferrari's Villoresi and Taruffi took fourth and fifth in a clean sweep of the points for the Scuderia.

### Entrants

Toulo de Graffenried returns to Alfa Romeo's four-car line-up in place of Paul Pietsch, having raced privately in France and Germany. He joins Fangio, Farina and Bonetto. Ferrari retain Ascari, Villoresi, González and Taruffi.

Brazilian Chico Landi makes his World Championship debut in a privately entered Ferrari. Works teams from BRM, Simca-Gordini and OSCA also compete, alongside the usual Talbot-Lago entries. Reg Parnell is listed for the BRM entry but is unable to start due to lubrication problems.

The front row sees Fangio, Farina, Ascari and González share the four fastest qualifying times between the two works teams.
