### Championship standings

Vettel leads the championship on 255 points after Abu Dhabi. Alonso is second on 245 — ten points back with two races remaining. Vettel can clinch the title at Austin if he wins and Alonso finishes fifth or lower.

### Previous race

Räikkönen won in Abu Dhabi with Alonso second and Vettel third — Räikkönen's first victory since Australia 2009 and Lotus F1's first win. The result trimmed Vettel's lead from 13 points to ten.

### Circuit

This is the inaugural Formula One race at the Circuit of the Americas, marking the return of the United States Grand Prix after a five-year absence — the last held at Indianapolis in 2007. The 5.513 km, 20-turn circuit overcame a stop-work order in November 2011 and threats of cancellation. Final tarmac was laid on 21 September and FIA approval granted four days later. A single DRS zone runs along the back straight into Turn 12.

### Tyre choices

Pirelli brings the silver-banded hard compound as the prime and white-banded medium as the option. Paul Hembery acknowledged after practice that soft and super-soft compounds would have better suited the circuit's characteristics; the conservative selection is expected to favour one-stop strategies.

### Practice

FP1: Vettel dominated with grip levels minimal throughout — his times were 1.5 seconds faster than Hamilton and two seconds ahead of Alonso. Ma Qinghua replaced Karthikeyan at HRT, finishing last, ten seconds behind Vettel.

FP2: Vettel again led despite a water leak that sidelined him for half the session, outpacing Webber and Alonso by 0.7 seconds. Kovalainen suffered a puncture after contact with Vergne; Maldonado retired with an engine failure.

FP3: Vettel completed the practice sweep, finishing 0.25 seconds ahead of Hamilton. Pérez and Pic collided, damaging Pic's floor. Vergne broke his front suspension hitting a kerb.
